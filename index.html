Background

What is Digital Product Passport?

A DPP is a verifiable digital record attached to a physical product — scan it, and you see what it's made of, where it came from, and its environmental footprint.

Under the EU's ESPR, the DPP becomes mandatory in the years ahead — the regulation, not a nice-to-have.

How it works

Four steps. No new infrastructure.

1

Input

The factory places its data in a secure workspace — visible to no one else.

2

Process

Our agent formats the data into the DPP schema — and encrypts anything you choose to keep private.

3

Upload

The DPP is published with cryptographic guarantees: tamper-proof, and even the hidden parts stay verifiable.

4

Verify

EU authorities verify the passport directly; consumers scan a QR code to see its public information.

Works alongside your existing systems — data in, passport out. No ERP overhaul, no process redesign.

Appendix

How does a zero-knowledge proof work?

A prover convinces a verifier that a statement is true — while revealing nothing beyond the fact that it's true.

This is what lets you prove your passport is genuine without handing over a single supplier name.
